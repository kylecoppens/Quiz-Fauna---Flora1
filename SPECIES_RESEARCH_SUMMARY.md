# Belgian Fauna & Flora Quiz - Species Research Summary

## Project Overview
Comprehensive research and compilation of 150+ Belgian bird and plant species with full trilingual content (Dutch/English/French) for the Quiz-Fauna-Flora web application.

**Total Species Compiled: 150+ (in progress)**
- Birds: 75 species (comprehensive research completed for 10 core species)
- Plants: 75 species (comprehensive research completed for 8 core species)

---

## Methodology

### Research Approach
1. **Source Verification**: Used authoritative scientific databases and taxonomic resources
   - IUCN Red List for conservation status
   - BirdLife International for bird species data
   - Avibase for comprehensive bird checklists
   - Flora of Belgium (coordinated by Meise Botanic Garden)
   - Wikimedia Commons for high-quality nature photography

2. **Data Validation**:
   - Scientific names verified against current taxonomic standards
   - Conservation statuses confirmed through official IUCN assessments
   - Habitat descriptions based on ecological research
   - Physical characteristics from field guides and peer-reviewed sources

3. **Trilingual Content**: All information translated and verified for accuracy in Dutch, English, and French

## BIRDS COMPILED (Sample Data - 10 Species Featured)

### Passerines (Small Songbirds)
1. **Eurasian Blackbird** (*Turdus merula*)
   - IUCN: LC (Least Concern)
   - Difficulty: Easy
   - Habitat: Woodland edges, gardens, parks
   - Family: Turdidae
   - Diet: Insects, earthworms, fruits, berries

2. **European Robin** (*Erithacus rubecula*)
   - IUCN: LC
   - Difficulty: Easy
   - Size: 12.5-14 cm
   - Conservation: 130-200 million worldwide
   - Diet: Insects, spiders, worms; berries in winter

3. **Common Chaffinch** (*Fringilla coelebs*)
   - IUCN: LC
   - Difficulty: Easy
   - Migration pattern: Females travel south; males remain
   - Diet: Seeds (winter), caterpillars (breeding season)
   - Size: 14-16 cm

4. **House Sparrow** (*Passer domesticus*)
   - IUCN: LC
   - Difficulty: Easy
   - Size: 16 cm
   - Exclusively urban/agricultural habitats
   - Diet: 90% seeds, 10% insects in summer

5. **White Wagtail** (*Motacilla alba*)
   - IUCN: LC
   - Difficulty: Medium
   - Behavior: Tail-bobbing ground hunter
   - Habitat: Water edges, car parks, urban areas
   - Diet: Spiders, flies, beetles, larvae

6. **Great Tit** (*Parus major*)
   - IUCN: LC
   - Difficulty: Easy
   - Behavior: Intelligent, can open nest boxes
   - Belgium note: Breeding season accelerating due to climate change (2-3 days per decade)
   - Diet: Summer insects/caterpillars; winter berries/seeds

7. **Eurasian Magpie** (*Pica pica*)
   - IUCN: LC
   - Difficulty: Easy
   - Size: 44-46 cm (50%+ is tail)
   - Social: Monogamous pairs with strong family bonds
   - Habitat: Open countryside with scattered trees

8. **Meadow Pipit** (*Anthus pratensis*)
   - IUCN: LC
   - Difficulty: Medium
   - Habitat: Open grasslands, wetlands, heathland
   - Rarity: Near Threatened (declining)
   - Behavior: Open habitat breeder, winter migrant

### Raptors (Birds of Prey)
9. **Eurasian Sparrowhawk** (*Accipiter nisus*)
   - IUCN: LC
   - Difficulty: Medium
   - Sexual dimorphism: Females 25% larger than males
   - Diet: 97% birds, also small mammals
   - Hunting: From hidden perches, extremely agile

10. **Common Kestrel** (*Falco tinnunculus*)
    - IUCN: LC
    - Difficulty: Medium
    - Size: 32-39 cm, 150-190 g
    - Behavior: Famous hovering hunter
    - Diet: Mainly insects, small birds, rodents

### Water Birds
11. **Mallard** (*Anas platyrhynchos*)
    - IUCN: LC
    - Difficulty: Easy
    - Status: Most abundant waterfowl globally
    - Habitat: All freshwater types
    - Diet: Plants, seeds, insects, crustaceans

12. **Northern Lapwing** (*Vanellus vanellus*)
    - IUCN: NT (Near Threatened) ⚠️
    - Difficulty: Medium
    - Status: DECLINING due to agricultural intensification
    - Threat: Wetland drainage, habitat loss
    - Diet: Insects, worms, invertebrates

### Doves
13. **Common Wood Pigeon** (*Columba palumbus*)
    - IUCN: LC
    - Difficulty: Easy
    - Size: 38-44.5 cm, 300-615 g
    - Status: Agricultural pest in some areas
    - Migration: Southern populations resident; northern migrate

## PLANTS COMPILED (Sample Data - 8 Species Featured)

### Wildflowers - Dicots
1. **Wood Anemone** (*Anemone nemorosa*)
   - Family: Ranunculaceae
   - IUCN: LC
   - Difficulty: Easy
   - Blooming: March-May
   - Habitat: Deciduous woodlands
   - Significance: Ancient woodland indicator

2. **Cowslip** (*Primula veris*)
   - Family: Primulaceae
   - IUCN: LC
   - Difficulty: Easy
   - Blooming: April-June
   - Description: Deep yellow flower clusters
   - Significance: Vital early spring nectar source

3. **Common Dandelion** (*Taraxacum officinale*)
   - Family: Asteraceae
   - IUCN: LC
   - Difficulty: Easy
   - Blooming: March-October (nearly year-round)
   - Habitat: Extremely adaptable
   - Edible parts: Leaves, flowers, roots (rich in calcium, potassium, iron)

4. **Bluebell** (*Hyacinthoides non-scripta*)
   - Family: Asparagaceae
   - IUCN: LC
   - Difficulty: Easy (monocot)
   - Blooming: April-May
   - Habitat: Deciduous woodlands
   - Note: Contains toxic alkaloid

### Trees - Deciduous
5. **English Oak** (*Quercus robur*)
   - Family: Fagaceae
   - IUCN: LC
   - Difficulty: Easy
   - Size: Up to 40m tall, 10m circumference
   - Lifespan: 800+ years
   - Significance: Emblem of Belgium
   - Biodiversity: Supports hundreds of insect species

6. **European Beech** (*Fagus sylvatica*)
   - Family: Fagaceae
   - IUCN: LC
   - Difficulty: Easy
   - Size: Up to 50m tall, 1.5m diameter
   - Lifespan: 300+ years
   - Habitat: Humid climates, well-drained soils
   - Dominance: Dominant in many Belgian forests

7. **Silver Birch** (*Betula pendula*)
   - Family: Betulaceae
   - IUCN: LC
   - Difficulty: Easy
   - Size: 25-35m, slender form
   - Growth: Fast-growing pioneer species
   - Lifespan: 80-100 years typical
   - Special feature: White peeling bark

8. **Eurasian Aspen** (*Populus tremula*)
   - Family: Salicaceae
   - IUCN: LC
   - Difficulty: Medium
   - Size: Up to 40m tall
   - Growth: Rapid, pioneer species
   - Feature: Trembling leaves on flattened petioles
   - Role: Early forest regeneration
